Film Sound Synchronization

Film sound synchronization refers to the process of aligning sound with visuals in early cinema, marking a significant milestone in the evolution of film music. In the earliest uses, filmmakers synchronized musical scores, sound effects, and dialogue with on-screen action to create a more immersive experience for the audience. This development allowed for a deeper emotional connection and more dynamic storytelling, paving the way for modern sound design techniques in cinema.
